Doctoral-Level Consultation Playbook

Organizational Development

  • How aligned are your strategic objectives with the daily decision-making of frontline employees?
  • If I asked three different teams what success looks like here, would their answers be consistent?
  • What mechanisms do you use to detect misalignment before it becomes costly?

Human Factors Engineering

  • Where in your processes do human errors or workarounds seem to repeat — and do you view them as individual failings or system design signals?
  • How much of your workflow design assumes people should adapt to the system, versus the system being designed around human limits?
  • If a new hire shadowed your current team, what parts of the system would feel intuitive, and what would feel unnecessarily complex?

Talent Management

  • How do you distinguish between turnover that is healthy versus turnover that signals disengagement or systemic issues?
  • In your workforce, do you see generational friction — and how is it expressed in meetings, promotions, or informal communication?
  • What signals do you look for that someone is thriving here — and how predictive are those signals of long-term retention?

Engineering Workflow Management

  • Where do projects most often stall — at handoffs, in approvals, or at the interface of disciplines?
  • How do you measure workflow efficiency — time, cost, quality, or resilience to change?
  • If one bottleneck could be removed tomorrow, what would it be — and why does it persist today?

Document Management

  • When employees need critical information, how long does it take them to find it — and how confident are they in its accuracy?
  • What compliance or audit risks have been most stressful in the last two years — and how much of that is traceable to documentation gaps?
  • If three key employees left tomorrow, what institutional knowledge would disappear — and what would remain accessible?

Change Management

  • When major changes occur here, what emotions do you notice most — confusion, skepticism, or fatigue?
  • How do leaders here frame change — as a burden, a necessity, or an opportunity?
  • What patterns have you observed in past change initiatives — what made the successful ones work, and why did others fail?

Algorithmic Psychology & Workforce Analytics

  • What’s the last workforce report you looked at — and what questions did it leave unanswered?
  • If I asked why your top performers stay, would your answer be based on data, anecdote, or assumption?
  • Which engagement signals do you wish you could predict — and what would you do differently if you had that foresight?

Closing Transition

  • From what we’ve discussed, which issues feel most urgent — and which are most critical for long-term sustainability?
  • If we designed a system to reduce waste, strengthen alignment, and build resilience, what would tangible success look like for you?
  • Would you prefer we start with a targeted intervention in one area, or a holistic diagnostic across multiple workforce dimensions?
